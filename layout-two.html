Spring in Japan (March–May) is a vibrant, popular season defined by mild weather, stunning cherry blossoms (sakura), and the "Golden Week" holiday, often requiring early booking due to high tourist volume. Temperatures rise from around 12°C in March to 20°C–25°C in May, offering a perfect, sunny backdrop for hanami (flower-viewing) picnics.

Cherry blossoms, known as sakura in Japan, are the fleeting, delicate pink and white flowers of trees in the Prunus genus. Symbolizing spring, renewal, and the impermanence of life, they bloom for only a few weeks, prompting the traditional Japanese custom of hanami (flower viewing) picnics.
